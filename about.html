About
me

I am a photography enthusiast who likes walking into new cities with a camera, a roll of film and enough curiosity to get lost for a while.

I love instant cameras because they are honest in a very charming way. Compared with digital cameras, Polaroid feels less like checking a result and more like receiving a small surprise. The moment I press the shutter, something ordinary becomes fixed. Then the image slowly appears, with different colors, marks and moods each time, almost like opening a blind box made by light.

I also love hiking and travelling, because the road gives every photo its own background story: a ferry wind, a mountain path, a crowded square, a table after dinner. This archive is where those fragments stay.

Finding No.25

Like the protagonist in The Secret Life of Walter Mitty searching for frame 25, I like to imagine that I am also looking for my own No.25 negative.

It might be in Rome, Istanbul, Milan, Turin, Amalfi, or somewhere without a famous name. It might be a perfect frame, or it might be a slightly blurred one that still remembers exactly how the day felt. Photography gives me a reason to keep looking, and every trip becomes part of that quiet search.
